德语时刻 | Jojo 与 Yoyo

Transcript | 节目原稿

Hallo, hier ist Jojo's Pod. 你好，这里是 Yoyo's Pod.

Hallo, this is Jojo's Pod. Hello, this is Yoyo's Pod.

欸，不是 Jojo 吗，怎么变成了 Yoyo？嗯，在德语里 Jojo 就会读作 Yoyo。

Wait, isn’t it Jojo? Why did it become Yoyo? Well, in German, Jojo is pronounced as Yoyo.

我曾经一直以为，字母 J 在人名永远只能读成 [d͡ʒ]，比如 John 约翰、Jack 杰克。后来我去西班牙时，才发现原来 J 还可以读成 [h]，比如 Juan 胡安、José 何塞。而仔细看法语里的人名，会发现 Jean 读作 [ʒ]，Jean Valjean [ʒɑ̃ val.ʒɑ̃]。现在才发现，在德语里 J 被读作 [j]，比如 Johannes。

I once thought the letter J in names could only be pronounced [d͡ʒ], like John or Jack. Later in Spain, I found J could also be [h], as in Juan or José. Looking at French names, J is [ʒ], like Jean Valjean [ʒɑ̃ val.ʒɑ̃]. Now I’ve realized that in German J is [j], as in Johannes.

其实 Johannes、Jean、Juan、John，这些名字都可以追溯到共同来源：古希腊语的 Ioannes，更久远到希伯来语的 Yohanan，意思是 “Jehovah has favored”，耶和华眷顾。所以这个名字有无数个变体：Johann, Hans, Jan…

In fact, Johannes, Jean, Juan, and John all trace back to a common origin: the Greek Ioannes, and further to the Hebrew Yohanan, meaning “Jehovah has favored.” That’s why there are countless variants: Johann, Hans, Jan…

你会发现，古希腊语和希伯来语字母表里都没有 J，只有 I（ι, iota）。古拉丁文里也没有 J，它其实是 I 的变体，直到中世纪才逐渐独立出来。

You’ll notice that neither the Greek nor Hebrew alphabets had J, only I (ι, iota). Latin also had no J; it was a variant of I until the Middle Ages, when it gradually became independent.

说回到 Jojo：我查了《Jojo Rabbit / 乔乔的异想世界》，电影里发音是 Jojo。虽然故事设定在德国，但毕竟是英语片。男孩本名 Johannes，在德语里小名自然是 Jojo，听起来像悠悠球，对啦，Jojo 在德语里的意思正是悠悠球。这让我想起，一个奇怪的人曾给我两个悠悠球，我怀疑他是故意的。

Back to Jojo: in the film *Jojo Rabbit*, it’s pronounced “Jojo.” Though set in Germany, it’s an English-language film. The boy’s real name is Johannes, and in German his nickname would be Jojo, which also means “yo-yo.” That reminded me of a strange person who once gave me two yo-yos—I suspect on purpose.

再说电影，开头的 Beatles《I Want to Hold Your Hand》竟然是德语版《Komm, gib mir deine Hand》。配合 Jojo 在个人崇拜中狂热暴走的画面，正好唱到 “Du nimmst mir den Verstand / You take away my reason / You drive me crazy”。讽刺又搞笑。而英语版没有这句，对应的是 “I think you’ll understand”。张力更大。不知道 Beatles 看到会有什么感想 :)

At the film’s start, the Beatles’ “I Want to Hold Your Hand” is sung in German: “Komm, gib mir deine Hand.” As Jojo rages in fanaticism, the line goes: “Du nimmst mir den Verstand / You take away my reason / You drive me crazy.” Satirical and hilarious. The English version lacks this, instead saying “I think you’ll understand.” That makes the contrast sharper. I wonder what the Beatles would think seeing their song used this way. :)

片尾则是 Bowie 的《Heroes》，原唱德语版《Helden》。结合创作背景，墙边的德语版更有切肤之痛。半个世纪后，有形无形的墙似乎并未减少，甚至对立更激烈。

The ending uses Bowie’s *Heroes*, originally sung in German as *Helden*. With its background, the German version by the wall feels even more poignant. Half a century later, visible and invisible walls seem no fewer—if anything, oppositions are sharper.

语言交错拓宽感受与认知的边界，编织出更广阔的理解，也孕育创造力。语言学习是利器，尤其对那个成长在自以为掌握绝对真理环境下的 Jojo。否则，他唱着 “Für das Recht und die Unschuld / For justice and innocence / 为了正义与纯真”，却只会成就眼前的罪恶。

The crossings of language expand our sensibility and understanding, weaving broader ground and nurturing creativity. Language learning is a tool, especially for one raised believing in absolute truth like Jojo. Otherwise, when he sings “Für das Recht und die Unschuld / For justice and innocence,” it only becomes the evil before his eyes.

爱因斯坦在《我的世界观》中写过：“一个人真正的价值首先取决于他在什么程度上和在什么意义上从自我解放出来。”我想，多样的语言正是从自我伸展出去的一种方式。

Einstein once wrote in *The World As I See It*: “The true value of a human being is determined primarily by the measure and the sense in which he has attained to liberation from the self.” I think diverse languages are one way of stretching beyond the self.

不同语言的对比让概念更丰富。比如 Jojo 与 Yoyo。我有时会想，如果汉族也在地中海边上，会发展出什么样的语言？那样就未必会有“相见时难别亦难，东风无力百花残”的婉转伤感，也不会有“君不见黄河之水天上来”的磅礴豪迈。所以我还是选择，跟随方块字探寻字母的世界，再跟随字母返回方块字的迷人。There and Back Again. Forever and Ever.

Such comparisons enrich concepts. Like Jojo and Yoyo. Sometimes I wonder: if the Han people had lived by the Mediterranean, what language would have developed? Then we might not have the lingering sorrow of “Hard to meet, harder to part, the east wind powerless, flowers falling,” nor the grandeur of “See you not, the Yellow River comes from heaven.” So I still choose to follow Chinese characters into the alphabetic world, then return with them to the charm of characters. There and Back Again. Forever and Ever.

今天以电影《Jojo Rabbit》片尾引用的里尔克诗句结束：

Today I’ll end with the lines from Rilke quoted at the end of *Jojo Rabbit*:

让一切发生在你身上：
美与惊惧。
只要继续前行——
没有哪种感受会是终点。

Let everything happen to you:
Beauty and terror.
Just keep going—
No feeling is final.

靠近的，就是那片他们称为“生命”的土地。
你将凭借它的庄严认出它。

Near is the land they call life.
You will know it by its gravity.

少年 Jojo 可以勇敢探索，Beauty and terror，无需畏惧。中年的 Jojo 们也许会更懂得，欢愉与悲伤，爱与痛，相互交织。生命因其深重而被感知，这重量让我们真正活着。

Young Jojo can explore bravely, facing beauty and terror without fear. Older Jojos may know better: joy and sorrow, love and pain, intertwined. Life is felt through its gravity, and this weight makes us truly alive.
