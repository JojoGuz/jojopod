00:22【无解的大小和弦感觉差异 / Major vs. minor – that strange difference】

有些问题似乎本来就是无解的。

比如，为什么大和弦(e.g.C-E-G 🎶)与小和弦(e.g.A-C-E 🎶)只有一个半音的分别，而在感受上却会有着那么明显的不同。 虽然在频率比和生理学上都有人尝试解释：大三度频率比约为5:4，在第20个采样点重合，小三度的频率比约为6:5，在第30个采样点重合，后者对于人类的神经系统意味着更复杂，再有，大和弦的频率比约为 4:5:6，小和弦的频率比约为 10:12:15，因此大和弦要更容易预测和把握，对比小和弦的紧张与微妙，它听起来会更和谐更明亮。

但我想我永远无法完全捕捉到自己的感受，所以也无法完全接受这一类解释。

Some questions seem destined to remain unsolved.

Like—why does a major chord, say C–E–G 🎶, feel so bright, while a minor one, A–C–E 🎶, feels so different, even though they’re only half a step apart? There are explanations from acoustics and physiology: the frequency ratio of a major third is about 5:4, overlapping at the 20th sampling point, while that of a minor third is 6:5, overlapping at the 30th. So the minor one is a bit more complex for our brains. And the full major triad, 4:5:6, is more predictable and stable than the minor one, 10:12:15. That’s why major chords sound clearer and happier, and minors feel more tense or subtle.

And yet, I know I’ll never be able to fully capture how I feel about it — so I can’t entirely accept those scientific explanations.

01:30【单独的音与关系中的音 / A single note and the note in relation】

对于音乐小白，比起练习曲目，我会更有兴趣在键盘上重复和变换简单的和弦结构，试着从中辨别不同的情绪感受，而这其实也是一件有点挑战又有点诡异的事。

我发现，每一个音单独听时，都有自己独特的气质。而当它与其他音形成关系时，会表现出明显被放大很多倍的鲜明特点；并且随着关系的不同，它的特点是多变的。也就是说，每一个单独的音都有自己极大的存在潜能，而多样的关系让它们的潜能被激活。(音乐人：这不就是调式吗) (G's adventure)

As a perpetual beginner in music, I often find more joy in repeating and transforming simple chord structures on the keyboard than in practicing full pieces. I try to sense the emotional differences that emerge from those small shifts. It’s oddly challenging — and a little uncanny.

I realized that every note, when heard alone, carries its own temperament. But once it forms a relationship with other notes, its character is amplified many times over — and it changes with each connection. So, every note has its huge potential, and through diverse connections that it comes alive. (Musician: “Isn’t that what modes are?”)(G’s Adventure)

02:14【向上展开的五度音序列 / The ascending fifth sequence】

有一天在摸索中，我试着接连按下几个纯五度音 (F2-C3-G3-D4-A4-E5 🎶)，瞬间，我感觉自己好像置身于一个巨大的空间里，大脑也似乎漂浮在一种冥想的状态，仿佛个体的小宇宙由此飘进了那个浩瀚的宇宙。是不是有点太神秘主义了。(音乐人：这不就是FCGDAE吗) (spiral to the Universe)

可能也不是。

One day, while experimenting, I played a chain of perfect fifths — F2, C3, G3, D4, A4, E5 🎶— and suddenly, it felt like I was floating in a huge space. My mind went quiet, almost like meditation, as if my own tiny universe was drifting into the great cosmic one. Perhaps that sounds a bit mystical. (Musician: “That’s just F–C–G–D–A–E.”)(Spiral to the Universe)

Maybe not.

02:45【频率的比例关系的发现与延伸 /The Discovery and Extension of Frequency Ratios】

据说公元前6世纪的一天，古希腊哲学家毕达哥拉斯散步路过一家铁匠铺，看到里面4个铁匠正在捶打烧红的金属。毕达哥拉斯听到每把锤子敲击到金属上的声音都不同，但听起来却大多和谐。于是他回到家就开始做实验，用一把古老的单弦琴，改变中间弦码的位置，发现了振动与音高的比例关系[1]。把弦码放在正中间时拨动琴弦，比未放弦码时的音，会高八度，频率比是2:1，弦码放在三分之二处时，拨长弦的音，比未放弦码时的音会高五度，频率比是3:2。

也就是说，五度音的比例接近黄金分割；那么连续的五度音，是不是会形成斐波那契 (Fibonacci c.1170-1250) 的黄金螺线呢？

It is said that one day in the 6th century BCE, the Greek philosopher Pythagoras was walking past a blacksmith’s workshop. He noticed four smiths hammering heated metal, and although the sounds of each hammer were different, they still seemed harmonious. Curious, he returned home and began to experiment with a monochord — an ancient one-stringed instrument. By shifting the bridge along the string, he discovered a relationship between vibration and pitch.

When the bridge was placed at the midpoint, the pitch doubled — one octave higher, with a frequency ratio of 2:1. When placed at two-thirds of the string, the longer part produced a tone a fifth above the open string, with a ratio of 3:2. So basically, the perfect fifth is really close to the golden ratio. Which made me wonder — if we keep stacking fifths, could they form something like the Fibonacci spiral?

03:43【【万物的生长螺线 / The Spiral of Growth in All Things】

还真差不多。黄金比 φ≈1.618，而这条五度音链的增长率基于3:2，约为1.5，这一音高频率系列[2]全部均匀地落在一条变化率近似黄金比例的对数螺线上，这样就不难解释在我听到它时会有一种特别的感受：

因为小到一只螺壳，大到宇宙里银河系的旋臂，都在各自的这样一条变化等于自身 (Eadem mutata resurgo) [3]的生命曲线上生长不息；

而这个纯五度音序列，在从自身展开的和声与泛音的叠加中，可以听到一个宇宙的宏大展开，并且因为E这个音，伸展向无限。

F2-C3-G3-D4-A4-E5 🎶

Indeed, almost. The golden ratio φ ≈ 1.618, while the growth rate of the fifth sequence, based on 3:2, is about 1.5. This means that the whole frequency series lies evenly on a logarithmic spiral whose rate of change nearly equals the golden proportion. Maybe that’s why it feels so special when I play it — like the sound itself is expanding.

From the smallest seashell to the vast spiral arms of galaxies, everything grows along a curve whose transformation equals itself — _Eadem mutata resurgo._

And within this pure sequence of fifths, through the expansion of harmonics and overtones, one can hear the grand unfolding of the universe — a sound that stretches infinitely, reaching outward with that final note, **E**.
